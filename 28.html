About Iligan City

Iligan, officially the City of Iligan, is a 1st class highly urbanized city in Northern Mindanao, Philippines. Known as the "City of Majestic Waterfalls," it boasts 23 stunning waterfalls, with Maria Cristina Falls being the most famous.

The city is also an industrial hub, earning the nickname "Industrial City of the South." Beyond its industrial might, Iligan offers rich cultural heritage, delicious cuisine, and breathtaking natural landscapes.

Diyandi Festival

The Diyandi Festival is Iligan City's founding anniversary celebration held every September 29. The name "Diyandi" comes from the Cebuano word meaning "to celebrate" or "merry-making."

Highlights include:

  • Street dancing competition featuring colorful costumes
  • Cultural shows and performances
  • Trade fairs and food festivals
  • Fireworks display

The festival showcases Iligan's rich cultural heritage and the unity of its diverse communities.

×

Hugyaw sa Iligan

Hugyaw sa Iligan is the city's annual Christmas festival known for its spectacular display of lights and festive celebrations. "Hugyaw" means jubilation in Cebuano.

Festival features:

  • Giant Christmas tree lighting ceremony
  • Parade of lights and lanterns
  • Christmas village with food stalls
  • Cultural performances and concerts

The event transforms Iligan into a magical wonderland during the holiday season.

×

Kawayan Festival

The Kawayan (Bamboo) Festival celebrates Iligan's bamboo industry and promotes environmental conservation. Held every October, it highlights the versatility of bamboo.

Festival activities include:

  • Bamboo craft exhibits and workshops
  • Bamboo cooking demonstrations
  • Bamboo planting activities
  • Cultural shows featuring bamboo instruments

The festival promotes sustainable use of bamboo as an eco-friendly resource.

Discover Iligan City's 23 Waterfalls

Explore the majestic waterfalls that give Iligan its nickname as the "City of Waterfalls"

Maria Cristina Falls

The most famous waterfall in Iligan, powering hydroelectric plants with its 98-meter drop.

Tinago Falls

Hidden gem accessible by 500 steps, featuring a deep blue lagoon at its base.

Mimbalot Falls

A serene three-tiered waterfall surrounded by lush greenery.

Abaga Falls

Known for its wide curtain-like cascade and refreshing natural pool.

Dodiongan Falls

A 20-meter high waterfall with a strong water flow, perfect for adventure seekers.

Limunsudan Falls

The highest waterfall in the Philippines with a two-tiered drop totaling 265 meters.

Guimbalawan Falls

A hidden waterfall with crystal-clear waters and a peaceful atmosphere.

Kalubihon Falls

Features multiple cascades and is surrounded by rich biodiversity.

Lumbatan Falls

A gentle waterfall surrounded by native flora and fauna.

Dalipuga Falls

A scenic waterfall located near an old Spanish-era settlement.

Langilanon Falls

A small but charming waterfall set in a lush green environment.

Hindang Falls

A popular spot for locals with refreshing waters perfect for swimming.

Pampam Falls

Known for its beautiful cascading waters and easy access trail.

Languyon Falls

Picturesque cascades surrounded by dense forest vegetation.

Linanot Falls

A hidden waterfall with crystal-clear pools and rich biodiversity.

Malinao Falls

Scenic and peaceful, perfect for relaxation and nature photography.

Malaigang Falls

A quiet waterfall with soothing sounds and pristine water pools.

Limunsudan Falls

Known for its wide cascade and accessibility for all ages.

Bridal Veil Falls

A beautiful waterfall with a calm pool ideal for swimming and picnics.
